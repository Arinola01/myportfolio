Health insurance coverage Analysis

This project focuses on evaluating the scope, effectiveness, and efficiency of health insurance coverage. This analysis helps policymakers, insurance providers, and consumers understand the extent of insurance coverage, and its impact on health outcomes, costs, and accessibility.

Key insights:
✪ In 2016, more states expanded their Medicaid programs to cover more low-income people.
✪ The average monthly tax credit in 2016 was about $300.
✪ California had the highest Medicare enrollment while Alaska had the lowest.

Recommendations
The government should focus on expanding coverage, improving affordability, ensuring equitable access to healthcare, and fostering innovation in the health insurance system. By addressing these areas, health insurance coverage can be more effective in meeting the needs of all populations, improving health outcomes, and controlling costs.
